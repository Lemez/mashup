Look at these this sentence:

Here are Tom's friends.

The letter S comes twice. When you put an S together with an apostrophe, like this - 'S - it shows us whose friends they are. The S in Tom's is called a possessive S, because it tells you who the friends belong to.

The second S does a different job. The S in friends tells us there is more than one friend.
Adding an S at the end of a noun like friend changes it from a singular word into a plural word: friends.
